üì¶ Dataset Overview: Olist Brazilian E-commerce

This project utilizes the Olist Brazilian E-commerce Public Dataset, a real-world dataset collected from an online marketplace platform called Olist. The platform connects small and medium businesses to customers across Brazil.

Originally released on Kaggle, this dataset enables a wide range of analytics and machine learning applications related to order processing, customer satisfaction, logistics, and seller performance.

üß© Key Files:

  • orders.csv: Core order information including order dates, customer IDs, and order status.
  • order_items.csv: Item-level details such as prices, freight charges, and product IDs.
  • products.csv: Metadata like product category, weight, and dimensions.
  • order_reviews.csv: Star ratings (1‚Äì5) and customer review comments.
  • customers.csv: Customer locations and IDs.
  • sellers.csv: Seller location data (city/state) and IDs.
  • geolocation.csv: ZIP code to GPS coordinates for mapping logistics.
  • order_payments.csv: Payment methods and installment details.

üìç Dataset Scope:

  • Time Period: January 2016 ‚Äì August 2018
  • Region: Nationwide coverage in Brazil
  • Language: Originally in Portuguese, with translated and cleaned metadata provided

üîç Use Cases:

  • E-commerce funnel analysis (from order placement to delivery)
  • Customer satisfaction tracking through reviews

  • Geospatial analysis of delivery coverage
  • Payment method breakdowns and customer segmentation

üß™ Unified Olist Dataset: Merging 9 Data Sources into a Single DataFrame

To enable rich analytics on customer experience, product performance, logistics, and geography, we merged all 9 raw datasets into a unified DataFrame using pandas in Python. This created a highly enriched dataset capturing information from product category to seller location.

  • olist_orders_dataset.csv: Base table containing order_id, customer_id, and timestamps
  • olist_customers_dataset.csv: Joined on customer_id to add customer_city and customer_state
  • olist_order_reviews_dataset.csv: Joined on order_id to bring in review_score and comments
  • olist_geolocation_dataset.csv: Aggregated by ZIP prefix to approximate lat/lng for city-state mapping
  • olist_order_items_dataset.csv: Merged on order_id to connect to product_id and seller_id
  • olist_products_dataset.csv: Merged on product_id for category and physical attributes
  • product_category_name_translation.csv: Used to translate product_category_name from Portuguese to English
  • olist_sellers_dataset.csv: Joined on seller_id to map seller location
  • olist_order_payments_dataset.csv: Added to provide payment method and installment details

Final dataset size: after all merges and deduplication, the unified DataFrame contains several thousand enriched orders, each with details on location, products, sellers, reviews, and payments.

üß† Customer Segmentation Using K-Means Clustering

To understand customer purchasing behavior, we applied K-Means clustering using three features: total spending, number of orders, and review score. To determine the optimal number of clusters, we used two key metrics:

  • WCSS (Within-Cluster Sum of Squares): measures compactness of clusters
  • Silhouette Score: measures separation between clusters

The plot above shows both metrics across k = 2 to 10. Based on the elbow point and silhouette peak, k = 6 was selected as optimal.

üìà Sales Predictions ‚Äì 6-Month Forecast

To anticipate future demand and guide strategic planning, we implemented a 6-month sales forecast using Facebook Prophet ‚Äî a time series forecasting library developed by Meta. It is widely used in business analytics because of its ease of use, ability to handle holidays and trend changes, and suitability for daily data.

üîß Methodology Overview

  • Tool Used: Facebook Prophet (Python)
  • Forecast Horizon: 180 days (‚âà 6 months)
  • Target: Top 5 product categories by total sales
  • Data Fields: ds = date, y = daily total sales per category
  • Seasonality: Enabled weekly and yearly patterns
  • Holiday Effects: Incorporated major Brazilian holidays (e.g., Black Friday, Christmas, Carnival)
  • Seasonality Mode: Set to 'additive' to capture linear trends

üìå Key Steps

  1. Parsed the original dataset and ensured the date column (ds) was in datetime format.
  2. Selected the top 5 categories based on total sales volume.
  3. Generated a holiday calendar to capture spikes or drops due to special events.
  4. Trained one Prophet model per product category using daily aggregated data.
  5. Generated 6-month forecasts and visualized daily projections.

These forecasts enable better inventory planning, budget allocation, and marketing scheduling. By identifying expected highs and lows in demand per category, stakeholders can proactively optimize supply chains, promotions, and resource deployment.
