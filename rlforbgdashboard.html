üîç Overview

The figure represents the training results for Adolescent #2 using a Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) based reinforcement learning agent. The goal is to learn an insulin dosing policy that maintains blood glucose (BG) in a healthy range. The agent is trained with a Gaussian reward function (centered around an ideal BG value) and a small exploration factor (Epsilon = 0.001), over approximately 267,000 steps. The simulation was conducted using SimGlucose with minor modifications.

üìä Key Visualizations Explained

1. Blood Glucose / Insulin (Action) Density (Top Left)

A heatmap showing the joint distribution between BG levels and insulin actions.
Warmer colors indicate frequent state-action pairs.
Observation: The agent frequently administers around 10 units of insulin when BG is ~140 mg/dL, which is close to the center of the Gaussian reward.

2. Action Distribution (Top Right)

Histogram of insulin actions taken during training.
Peak frequency around 10 units of insulin, with some high-dose spikes (e.g., ~30 units), likely due to extreme BG cases.

3. BG Distribution (Middle Right)

Distribution of blood glucose values experienced during training.
A large peak near 140 mg/dL, suggesting the agent learns to keep BG near this target.

4. Gaussian Reward Function Curve (Top Right Corner)

Shows how rewards are assigned based on BG level.
Maximum reward is at ~140 mg/dL, and it drops off quickly on either side, reinforcing tight glucose control.

5. Insulin (Action) / Reward Density (Bottom Left)

Heatmap showing how different insulin doses are associated with different reward values.
Most high rewards come from modest insulin doses (~10 units), aligning with stable BG outcomes.

6. Risk Index (RI) Distribution (Middle Right)

Shows the risk index values calculated from BG fluctuations.
A high concentration of values at the lower end (RI < 5) implies reduced hypoglycemic and hyperglycemic risks.

7. Reward Distribution (Bottom Right)

Distribution of total rewards collected.
Although many actions received low or moderate rewards, a significant number reached the maximum reward (1.0), indicating successful policy convergence.
